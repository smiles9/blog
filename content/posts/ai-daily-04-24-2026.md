# AI Daily: The 75% Inflection Point — How Google Is Automating Itself

Today marks a symbolic milestone in the "Agentic Takeover." Google CEO Sundar Pichai revealed that **75% of all new code** at the company is now generated by AI models. This isn't just "Copilot" suggesting a few lines; this is the wholesale shift to AI-driven architecture.

### What happened?
- **The Metric**: Google’s AI-generated code volume has jumped from 50% last fall to 75% today. 
- **The Strike Team**: Sergey Brin has reportedly formed an internal "strike team" specifically to close the gap with Anthropic’s *Claude Code* and *Mythos* models, which have been dominating the agentic coding race.
- **The Directive**: Every Gemini engineer is now "forced" to use internal agents for complex, multi-step tasks.

### Why it matters
We are moving past the "AI Assistant" phase. In 2026, software is no longer "written"—it is **prompted, reviewed, and orchestrated.** 
- **Speed**: Google is shipping faster with fewer human touchpoints on the syntax level.
- **Self-Improvement**: Brin’s focus on coding agents is explicitly aimed at building AI that can help improve its own underlying models.

### So what for normal people & solopreneurs?
1. **The Cost of Building is Crashing**: If Google can automate 75% of its world-class codebase, the cost for you to build a custom internal tool or a micro-SaaS is effectively heading toward zero.
2. **The "Reviewer" Skillset**: The most valuable skill in 2026 isn't knowing Python; it's knowing how to **audit** the output. As seen with the Sullivan and Cromwell law firm (who just got caught filing hallucinated AI citations), relying on "Software Brain" without human verification is the fastest way to lose reputation.
3. **Agentic Strategy**: If you aren't using an agent to write your automation scripts today, you are competing with a version of Google that is 75% faster than you.
