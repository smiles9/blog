### What happened
Recent reports from top-tier AI startup founders and industry analysts suggest a paradigm shift in how technology companies scale. A prominent $12 billion AI startup founder recently noted that the next generation of "tech giants" could potentially operate with fewer than 100 employees. This trend is driven by advances in AI that enable lean teams to manage millions of users and complex operations that previously required thousands of staff members.

### Why this matters
The traditional "blitzscaling" model, which often involved massive hiring to capture market share, is being challenged by "intelligence scaling." By integrating AI into every layer of the business - from customer support and marketing to product development and infrastructure management - companies can maintain high agility and low overhead while achieving massive reach. This levels the playing field significantly between established incumbents and nimble newcomers.

### So what for normal people / solopreneurs / small teams
For individuals and small teams, this is the ultimate validation of the "one-person business" or "micro-agency" model. You no longer need a large payroll to build a global product. 

- Focus on Leverage: Instead of thinking about "who" to hire, think about "what" to automate.
- Quality over Quantity: A small team of high-output individuals using AI can now outperform a large, traditional department.
- Profit over Revenue: By keeping costs extremely low through AI efficiency, solopreneurs can achieve profit margins that were previously impossible at scale.

The barrier to entry for building a "giant" has never been lower, provided you master the tools of the trade.
