### What happened

Alibaba has released its Qwen 3.5 series, marking a significant leap in on-device AI capabilities. The standout from this new release is the Qwen 3.5-2B model, which is small enough to run locally on a modern iPhone with just 4GB of RAM-even in airplane mode. Meanwhile, the slightly larger 9B variant is reportedly matching the performance of models thirteen times its size on key benchmarks.

### Why this matters

The "Model War" is shifting from just being bigger to being smarter and more efficient. For a long time, high-performance AI required massive cloud computing power and a constant internet connection. Alibaba's latest push demonstrates that the gap between massive frontier models and lightweight, local models is shrinking. This allows for faster, more private, and lower-cost AI operations that don't depend on expensive API subscriptions or stable connectivity.

### So what for normal people / solopreneurs / small teams

For solopreneurs and small teams, this is a game-changer for leverage and privacy:

- Local Intelligence: You can now run sophisticated AI tasks directly on your mobile device or laptop without sending sensitive client data to the cloud.
- Cost Efficiency: As small models match the performance of yesterday's giants, the cost of automation drops significantly. You can build "mini-employees" that run locally for free.
- Reliability: Since these models work offline, your AI-powered workflows won't break when you are traveling or in areas with poor connection.

One person with a well-configured local AI setup can now handle operations that previously required a dedicated small team.
