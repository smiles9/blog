### 🚀 The 75% Inflection Point
Google CEO Sundar Pichai dropped a bombshell: **75% of all new Google code is now AI-generated.** This is up from 50% just six months ago. While Google is using a "strike team" to catch up to Anthropic—which reportedly writes 70-90% of its code with Claude—the implications for the future of software engineering are clear. We aren't just writing code anymore; we are auditing and orchestrating it.

### 🛡️ Anthropic's 'Mythos' Finds 271 Firefox Bugs
Anthropic’s cybersecurity-focused model, Claude Mythos Preview, successfully identified 271 bugs in Firefox 150. Mozilla CTO Bobby Holley noted the model is "every bit as capable" as elite human researchers. This marks a significant step in AI-driven security auditing, though the "elite human" remains the benchmark for now.

### 🧠 OpenAI GPT-5.5 Rumors
OpenAI has hinted that GPT-5.5 is its "smartest and most intuitive" model yet. While details remain sparse, the anticipation is building for a model that moves beyond pattern matching into deeper logical reasoning. 

### ⚖️ AI vs. The FDA?
RFK Jr. claimed in congressional hearings that AI could make the FDA "irrelevant" by developing personalized medicine for every citizen. While controversial, it highlights the growing tension between legacy regulatory bodies and the rapid acceleration of AI-driven biotech.

### 🎵 Spotify's AI Labels
Spotify is rolling out voluntary AI labels for tracks, starting with DistroKid. As AI uploads threaten to overwhelm human creators, the battle for "proof of human" in art is officially underway.
